## SECTION 4: WHY THT STILL MATTERS

### H2: When Through Hole Is the Right Choice

**Mechanical Robustness**
Through-hole solder joints physically wrap around a plated barrel and form a meniscus on both sides of the board â€” creating a solder connection with significantly higher mechanical pull strength than a surface mount pad. For components subjected to repeated mating/unmating cycles (connectors), vibration (automotive, industrial), or thermal cycling, THT provides the durability that keeps connections intact.

**High-Current Applications**
Power components that carry substantial current generate heat â€” and the large solder fillets of through-hole connections provide better thermal mass and lower contact resistance than their surface-mount equivalents, improving power handling and long-term reliability.

**Legacy & Retrofit Manufacturing**
Many proven product designs use through-hole components that have been validated and field-proven over years of service. Revalidating a complete SMT redesign may be unnecessary and costly when the existing THT design continues to perform reliably.

**Mixed-Signal Integrity**
In some high-precision analogue and mixed-signal designs, through-hole component mounting provides superior noise immunity and ground plane connection compared to surface-mount alternatives â€” particularly for power supply decoupling and filtering.
